Effects of Bike Riding Characteristics on their Duration¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation I wanted to look at the characteristics of bike rides that could be used to predict their duration in seconds.The main focus was on the member_birth_year,member gender,start day,user type and bike share for all trip.

Dataset Overview¶

There are 183,412 fordbike rides in the dataset with 16 features (duration_sec,start_time,end_time,start_station_id, start_station_latitude,start_station_longitude,end_station_id,end_station_latitude,end_station_longitude,bike_id, member_birth_yea,member_gender and bike_share_for_all_trip). Most variables are numeric in nature, but the variables,duration_sec, start_time,end_time, and member_birth_year are ordered factor variables, having year starting from 1878 to 2001 and start_time and end_time these would need to be changed to datetime and int respectively, to give us the days of the week the rides took place which could be from monday to sunday,and the hours they took place aswell in a 24hour duration.

Distribution of Duration seconds¶

Duration in sec has a tall unimodal distribution, with most ride count occuring just alittle above 300 to 800 duration seconds values, and very few rider count in duration seconds from 3000 to 4000 seconds. When plotted on a log-scale, the duration_sec distribution shows a unimodal distribution with a clear peak between 300 and 800 in duration in secs. Interestingly, there's a steep gradual decrease in duration right after 800, rather than a smooth ramp up.

Distribution of Member Birth year¶

The member_birth_year is skewed to the left with the peak shifting towards the right hand side of the plot,here the mode>median>mean. The plot shows a little rough bimodal distribution having two peaks between the year 1990 and 2000.This means that the most rides occurr within these member_birth_year as they seem to be the working professionals in this dataset category.

Distribution of Hours with the Longest Duration¶

The utilisation of bike rides tends to be lowest at 3am and increases throughout the day starting at about 6am. To put it another way, people who ride bikes during the day do so for longer duration which is from 6am-9pm.

Distribution of Member Gender Bike Ride¶

Male and female exhibit the same traits during the course of the day, once more. So it's fair to say that women are less likely to utilise bicycles at night based on the empty gaps on the female map on the left than men.So from this plot men ride bikes at night for longer duration than women.

Distribution of Duration log of Rides By Start day,User type,Member gender and Bike for all share Trip¶

Plotting a bivariate exploation of duration log against start_day,user_type,member_gender and bike for all share trip shows that women tend to bike for longer periods of time than males do on average when I take into account the box plot and the violin plot. I notice that the violin plot for female members appears to be considerably raised when comparing member gender against duration sec. Men use the shorter duration more often.

The plot also shows that Saturday and Sunday have a somewhat different violin shape than the other days of the week on the plot of start hour against start day. This indicates that, in contrast to weekdays, when people use the bike sharing system in the morning and after work in the evenings, people utilise it mostly in the afternoons on weekends. Namely, 9am to 9pm.

It also shows that persons who use the bike share for all trips are often younger than those who don't by comparing the bike share for all trips against age.

Those who do not utilise the bike share for all journeys have a tendency to fall into one of two utilisation time frames when comparing the bike share for all trips versus the start hour. evenings and early mornings. The majority of those who do, nevertheless, are active in the nights. As a result, we can conclude that subscribers are most active at night.
